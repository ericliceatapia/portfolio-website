I am Eric Licea Tapia.

I was born in 2004 in Dallas, Texas. I am a first-generation Mexican-American college student currently studying at Texas Christian University.

My roots

As the oldest of three children to immigrant parents, I deeply value the sacrifices they made to provide a better future for us. My family comes from Los Rodriguez, a small town outside of San Miguel de Allende, Guanajuato, Mexico. Their resilience and dedication inspire me, and I strive to honor their journey in everything I do.

My interests

I love exploring new hobbies, but my current top three are exercising, listening to music, and tinkering with technology. I enjoy activities that challenge me physically, mentally, and intellectually. Some argue that passively engaging in activies such as listening to music or watching movies isn't a "real hobby," but I believe it's about how you interact with an activity rather than just doing it.

My goals

As a developer

My goal is to craft timeless, beautiful, and functional code--code that not only functions seamlessly today but shapes the future of technology. I aim to push boundaries, merging creativity with technical excellence to build code that is maintainable, scalable, and adaptable for the future.

In school

I see education as a powerful tool. My goal is to uplift and empower students from similar backgrounds, helping them realize their potential and use their time in school as a launchpad for greatness. I want to create a path for others to follow and prove that our backgrounds do not define our limits.

Professionally

My ambition is to lead and inspire. I want to grow into a position where I can drive innovation, challenge conventional thinking, and lead groundbreaking teams in the technology industry. My vision is to contribute to solutions that redefine how we interact with the modern world.

In life

I envision a life of depth, purpose, and connection. A life where I am free to explore, learn, and grow. I hope to understand my roots, embrace my heritage, and live in Mexico, immersing myself in the stories and traditions that shaped my family. Ultimately, I want to build a life where my future children inherit a legacy of curiosity, ambition, and resilience, just as my parents did for me.
